Zachary Schoenberger

I am an information specialist with a background in Library Science and Digital Humanities. I fancy myself a Python generalist and a perpetual student in the area of data management. I am currently working as a core developer on the LINCS Project, building a linked data infrastructure to support publishing and engagement with Canadian Humanitites linked data sets.

I get excited by all things data, especially data visualization, linked data, network analysis, and using scripting languages like R and Python to solve problems (or, to create new ones!).

I am interested in the intersection between information services and the digital humanities. I think a lot about how librarians and digital humanities can work together, how we might learn from our respective practices, and I wonder, where are we heading? This interest is informed by my MLIS and MA in Digital Humanities, my ongoing research activites, and my practical experience as a Metadata Analyst. A few years ago, I finished a Masters thesis on metadata quality in online participatory environments. I continue to refine my understanding of data quality through my work with Linked Data.
